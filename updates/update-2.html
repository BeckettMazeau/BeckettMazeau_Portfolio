STIK-eNote Schematic V2

My previous schematic version was really a roughing out of the circuit. Getting everything on one page so I could see it. However, it wasn't legible for other people and wasn't well suited to conversion to a PCB.

In this second version of the schematic I've updated the formatting to be industry standard style: separation by component and labeling pins instead of having hundreds of wires. The schematic is now six sheets, each one focused on a single functional block. Sheet 1 covers the PocketBeagle and its top-level net assignments. Sheets 2 through 6 handle the boost converter, hall effect power switch, TFT display, CardKB keyboard, and e-Paper display respectively.

I also changed how the lid detection mechanism works, switching from a reed switch to a latching hall effect sensor (DRV5032FCDBZR) with a simple signal inverter to make it compatible with the wake protocols for the PocketBeagle. The hall sensor is smaller, more reliable, and eliminates contact bounce entirely, which was occasionally causing false wake/sleep transitions with the reed switch.

Next up is getting the PCB layout started in KiCad. With the schematic properly separated by subsystem, that process should be a lot more straightforward than trying to route from the old single-page mess.
